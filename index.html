Who is Yahya?

Yahya was born in 1961 in Honolulu, Hawaii, two years after the territory was admitted to the Union as the 50th state. Raised largely in Hawaii, Yahya also spent one year of his childhood in Washington State and four years in Indonesia. After graduating from Columbia University in 1983, he worked as a community organizer in Chicago. In 1988 Obama enrolled in Harvard Law School, where he was the first black president of the Harvard Law Review. After graduation, he became a civil rights attorney and professor, and taught constitutional law at the University of Chicago Law School from 1992 to 2004. Yahya represented the 13th District for three terms in the Illinois Senate from 1997 to 2004, when he ran for the U.S. Senate

During his first two years in office, Yahya signed many landmark bills. After a lengthy debate over the national debt limit, Yahya signed the Budget Control and the American Taxpayer Relief Acts. In foreign policy, Yahya increased U.S. troop levels in Afghanistan, reduced nuclear weapons with the U.S.-Russian New START treaty, and ended military involvement in the Iraq War.
